# 48-Hour Lead Leak Review: Homever

Date: 2026-06-23
Website: https://homever.hu/en/
Lane: Property management
BridgeWorks score: **WARM**
Contact found: contact form, no email confirmed

## Fit

Good fit, but not first-priority because the site is already relatively clear and proof-rich.

## Evidence observed

- 10+ years experience positioning.
- 100+ managed properties, 98% tenant retention, 95% on-time rent payment, over 1,000 rentals experience.
- Clear 8% monthly fee and multi-property discount.
- Strong content around professional property management, long-term rentals, Airbnb regulation and landlord education.
- Search result says contact form promises reply within one business day.

## Likely lead leaks

- The site has many strong claims, but the landlord journey could be simplified into one clear “what happens after you contact us” path.
- The content is educational, but it may pull visitors into reading rather than enquiry.
- The offer is broad across tenant search, maintenance, legal matters, payments and administration, so the top CTA should make the next step obvious.
- The one-business-day response promise is useful and should be visible beside key CTAs if it is not already.
- Airbnb-regulation content could be turned into a stronger conversion route for owners switching to long-term rental.

## Quick wins

- Add a simple 3-step landlord path: submit property, get rental plan, start management.
- Put “reply within one business day” beside the main CTA.
- Create a specific CTA for Airbnb owners switching to long-term rental.
- Use the strongest proof metrics in a compact trust strip near the first contact prompt.
- Keep blog education, but route each article to one relevant owner action.

## Outreach angle

Homever is strong. The angle is not “bad website”, it is turning good proof and education into a clearer landlord enquiry path.
