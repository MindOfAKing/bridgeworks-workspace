# BridgeWorks Market Research Framework

Purpose: choose niche and location from evidence, not vibes.

Use this before building prospect lists or outreach campaigns.

## Default question

Which niche/location combination gives BridgeWorks the best chance of booking useful conversations with owner-led businesses that have visible lead leaks and enough contract value to pay?

## Research units

Score combinations, not isolated niches.

Example combinations:

- Budapest facility management
- Budapest dental clinics
- Pest County property services
- Vienna cleaning companies
- Hungary security services
- Budapest restaurants/hospitality proof lane

## Scorecard

Score each criterion 1-5.

| Criterion | Weight | What to look for |
|---|---:|---|
| Business density | 15 | Enough businesses in the location to build a list of 50-100 prospects |
| Ability to pay | 20 | Higher contract value, recurring revenue, B2B/commercial clients, not purely low-margin consumer traffic |
| Visible lead leaks | 20 | Weak website, unclear offer, poor Google presence, few reviews, hidden contact path, slow follow-up risk |
| Owner reachability | 15 | Owner/founder/contact person can be found through site, LinkedIn, Google, directories, or warm network |
| Urgency of problem | 10 | Enquiries matter commercially and poor trust/contact flow likely costs revenue |
| BridgeWorks fit | 10 | Problems map to website clarity, trust proof, follow-up, simple automation, digital foundation |
| Proof/referral leverage | 5 | Existing case study, local presence, relationship, language/cultural advantage |
| Delivery repeatability | 5 | Similar problems repeat across prospects, allowing templates and SOPs |

Maximum weighted score: 500.

## Decision bands

- 400-500: Test immediately. Build first 25 prospects.
- 320-399: Promising. Validate with 10 prospects and 3 outreach messages.
- 240-319: Watchlist. Needs sharper angle or better location.
- Under 240: Do not pursue now.

## Evidence sources

Use at least 3 evidence sources before choosing a focus.

### 1. Local business density

Sources:
- Google Maps / browser review
- OpenStreetMap via Hermes maps skill
- local directories
- chambers/associations
- LinkedIn company search

Evidence to capture:
- estimated count of businesses
- common locations/neighborhoods
- whether websites/contact pages exist

### 2. Ability to pay

Signals:
- B2B service or higher-ticket local service
- recurring contracts
- commercial clients
- multiple locations or team size
- professional website already exists, but underperforming
- service pages show valuable jobs/projects

Red flags:
- low-margin, discount-driven, owner stretched thin
- lots of tiny one-person businesses with no clear budget
- content-hungry but low retainer potential

### 3. Visible lead leaks

Check 10 sample businesses.

Look for:
- unclear homepage promise
- no strong call to action
- hidden phone/email/contact path
- weak Google Business Profile/reviews
- poor mobile experience
- no proof of work/results
- service pages are vague
- no follow-up path after enquiry

### 4. Reachability

Look for:
- owner/founder name
- email or contact form
- LinkedIn profile
- WhatsApp/phone
- company registration/directories
- warm connection possibility

### 5. BridgeWorks delivery fit

Can BridgeWorks realistically help within 2-4 weeks?

Good fit:
- lead path clarity
- website upgrade
- trust proof cleanup
- Google/contact journey improvements
- simple follow-up automation
- monthly care/review

Poor fit:
- needs regulated ads strategy only
- needs enterprise CRM replacement
- needs heavy custom software first
- needs constant content production but cannot pay

## Research sprint workflow

### Step 1: Generate candidate combinations

Start with 8-12 combinations from:
- Emmanuel's local access: Budapest, Hungary, CEE
- higher-value service niches
- existing proof lane: Oliviks/hospitality
- warm network possibilities

### Step 2: Desk research

For each combination:
- estimate business density
- inspect search results and directories
- sample 5-10 websites
- note visible lead leaks
- note likely ability to pay

### Step 3: Score

Use the weighted scorecard.

### Step 4: Select test lane

Pick:
- 1 primary test lane
- 1 backup lane
- 1 proof lane

### Step 5: Build first list

For the primary lane:
- add 25 prospects to tracker
- score each prospect HOT/WARM/COLD
- draft 5 personalized outreach messages
- send only after Emmanuel approves

## Recommended subagent split

Use parallel subagents when researching.

1. Market density agent
- Finds candidate businesses and location density.
- Uses maps, browser, web search.

2. Pain/pay signal agent
- Reviews sample websites and checks whether the niche has visible lead leaks and ability to pay.

3. Outreach angle agent
- Turns findings into positioning, lead leak hypotheses, and first-message angles.

Main Hermes agent merges their findings into the scorecard and updates the prospect tracker.
