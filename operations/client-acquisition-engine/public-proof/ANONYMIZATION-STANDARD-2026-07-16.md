# BridgeWorks Client-Proof Anonymization Standard

Date: 2026-07-16
Owner: Emmanuel Ehigbai

## Three proof tiers

1. Private evidence: named source records, client correspondence, screenshots, domains, contracts, invoices, and audit files.
2. Anonymized public proof: generalized client identity, verified outcome, method, limitations, and BridgeWorks interpretation.
3. Named public proof: used only when written client permission and the final publication asset are both recorded.

## Remove from anonymized proof

- Client names, legal entities, logos, domains, email addresses, phone numbers, and named contacts.
- Recognizable screenshots, branded labels, direct links, internal file paths, and source-system IDs.
- Invoice values, contract terms, private correspondence, and unneeded delivery-package fingerprints.
- Testimonials, quotes, or likenesses without recorded permission.
- File metadata and filenames that disclose the client identity.

## Retain only when defensible

- A verified outcome with its measurement boundary.
- A broad sector and region when they do not make the client readily identifiable.
- The method, limitations, unresolved items, and commercial lesson.
- Dates only at the minimum precision needed to support the claim.

## Publication rule

An anonymized asset still requires Emmanuel approval before publication. If reasonable public searching can identify the client from the remaining details, generalize the sector, location, timing, or delivery counts further.
