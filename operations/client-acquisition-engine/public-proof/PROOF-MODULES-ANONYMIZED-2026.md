## Proposal proof section

### Relevant BridgeWorks proof: anonymized B2B services engagement

The client came to BridgeWorks with strong offline capability and weak digital discoverability. The business had limited structured content, poor AI readability, and no systematic lead-generation infrastructure.

BridgeWorks built the digital foundation: website infrastructure, schema, sitemap coverage, crawler controls, reporting, content assets, and outbound preparation. By the engagement-close audit, the verified BridgeWorks diagnostic had improved from 16/100 to 77/100.

The useful lesson is not "more marketing." It is that buyers, search engines, and AI systems need enough structured evidence to understand what a company does and why it should be trusted.

## Objection handling

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "We already have a website." | This client also needed more than a visible website. The issue was whether the business was structured, crawlable, citable, and commercially clear. |
| "We just need ads." | Ads can amplify a weak foundation. The client needed the foundation first: pages, schema, content, sitemap, and lead-path clarity. |
| "AI visibility sounds vague." | The engagement made it concrete: measure entity clarity, schema, sitemap coverage, crawler access, business profiles, and citable service content. |
| "We do not have time for a big project." | The first step can be a focused scan. The scan shows whether the constraint is website structure, local profiles, reviews, content, or lead capture. |
