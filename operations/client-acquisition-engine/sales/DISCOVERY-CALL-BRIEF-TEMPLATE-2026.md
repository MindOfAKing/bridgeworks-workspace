## Call Structure

### 0 to 3 minutes: Context

- What made this worth discussing now?
- What would make this conversation useful for you?

### 3 to 10 minutes: Desired Outcome and Consequence

- What should the website or enquiry process do better?
- Where do new enquiries come from today?
- What happens after someone contacts you?
- Where do prospects hesitate, disappear, or ask the same questions?
- What does that cost in time, lost opportunities, or owner attention?

### 10 to 17 minutes: Current System and Constraints

- Who owns the website, domain, profiles, analytics, and content?
- What has already been tried?
- What must not be disrupted?
- Are there legal, brand, language, or platform constraints?
- Who else needs to approve the work?
- Is there a target date?

### 17 to 21 minutes: Investment and Fit

- Is this a focused fix, a broader foundation project, or still a diagnostic question?
- Have you set an investment range for solving it?
- Would a one-time project or ongoing support fit better?

Do not force a range if the problem is still unclear. Do not avoid the question when a proposal is being considered.

### 21 to 25 minutes: Recap and Next Step

Recap:

> I heard that `<problem>` is causing `<business consequence>`, and the priority is `<desired outcome>` by `<timing>`. The main constraints are `<constraints>`. Is that accurate?

Select one next step:

- Paid 48-Hour Lead Leak Review.
- Focused implementation proposal.
- Digital Foundation proposal.
- Follow-up-system scoping call.
- Nurture on a specific date.
- No fit and close.

Never promise a proposal before the fit and scope are clear.
