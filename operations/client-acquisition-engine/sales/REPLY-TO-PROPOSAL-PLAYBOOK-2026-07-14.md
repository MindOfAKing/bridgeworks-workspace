# Reply-to-Proposal Playbook

Date: 2026-07-14
Owner: Emmanuel Ehigbai
Status: Internal operating guide. No external action is authorized by this file.

## Purpose

Convert a genuine prospect reply into a qualified conversation, a clear next step, and a proposal only when the evidence supports one.

This is the manual workflow to prove before automating it.

## Operating Definitions

| Stage | Count it only when |
|---|---|
| Positive reply | The prospect expresses interest, asks a relevant question, requests the observations, or proposes a next step. An auto-reply does not count. |
| Qualified conversation | BridgeWorks speaks with a real business and confirms a relevant problem, business consequence, decision path, and agreed next step. |
| Proposal issued | A prospect-specific proposal has been reviewed by Emmanuel and sent to the prospect. A local draft does not count. |
| Engagement closed | Scope and commercial terms are accepted and the required initial payment is received, unless a signed contract explicitly defines another start condition. |

## Stage 1: Classify the Reply

| Reply type | Action | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Requests the three observations | Recheck the live evidence, send the concise observation note, and offer a 20-minute call. | Same business day where practical. |
| General interest | Ask one problem question and offer two calendar-checked call times. | Same business day where practical. |
| Asks for price | Give a relevant range with its scope boundary, then ask the minimum questions needed to recommend a level. | Same business day where practical. |
| Not now | Ask permission to follow up in a specific month and record that date. | Close the current sequence. |
| Referral | Thank the referrer, confirm permission to use their name, and research the referred company before contact. | Before any introduction message. |
| Negative or unsubscribe | Acknowledge once, close, and do not continue the sequence. | Immediately. |
| Auto-reply | Record the return date if useful. Do not count it as a reply or conversation. | No manual response unless needed. |

## Stage 2: Recheck Before Responding

For any requested observation:

1. Open the exact live page again.
2. Confirm the issue still exists.
3. Remove any observation that has been fixed or cannot be reproduced.
4. Keep the note to three high-value points.
5. Separate observed facts from BridgeWorks recommendations.
6. Do not attach the CEEFM PDF until Emmanuel approves that asset for external use.

## Stage 4: Qualify Before Proposing

A proposal is justified only when the conversation establishes:

- A real company and accountable contact.
- A problem BridgeWorks can solve.
- A business consequence worth addressing.
- The person or process that approves the work.
- A plausible timing window.
- Budget comfort or permission to discuss an investment range.
- Access to the site, profiles, analytics, or people needed for delivery.
- One agreed next step.

## Stage 5: Map the Offer

| Confirmed need | Primary offer | Do not sell yet when |
|---|---|---|
| Wants a narrow public-evidence AI-visibility snapshot | Free AI Visibility Scan, only while the public offer's scope and capacity are confirmed | The prospect expects a full website, trust, or enquiry-path audit for free. |
| Wants evidence before committing | 48-Hour Lead Leak Review, EUR 99 to EUR 250 | The prospect expects a full implementation plan for free. |
| Trust, copy, proof, or enquiry-path problem | Lead Path Fix, EUR 500 to EUR 1,500 | The underlying site cannot support a focused fix. |
| Website foundation is weak or missing | Website upgrade or Digital Foundation | Ownership, access, or scope is unclear. |
| Follow-up is manual and leads are being lost | Simple Follow-Up System | The business has no stable enquiry process to automate. |
| Needs ongoing improvements after a defined fix | Monthly Care | The project outcome and reporting rhythm are not agreed. |

Prices are internal guidance from `../../lead-engine-v1/04-offers/bridgeworks-offer-ladder.md`. Confirm the final price before a proposal leaves BridgeWorks.

## Stage 6: Use Proof at the Right Moment

Use CEEFM after the prospect's problem is understood, not as a generic trophy.

Approved factual frame:

> BridgeWorks delivered a March-to-June 2026 digital-growth foundation for CEEFM, a Budapest facility-management company. The verified engagement-close audit moved from 16/100 to 77/100 on BridgeWorks' GEO and AI-visibility diagnostic after website, structured-data, crawlability, content, reporting, and outbound-foundation work.

Always add the boundary:

> That is evidence of the CEEFM engagement, not a promise of the same score or commercial result for another business.

Proof mapping:

| Prospect issue | CEEFM proof |
|---|---|
| Buyers cannot understand the offer | Service structure and clearer website messaging. |
| AI/search systems cannot interpret the business | Schema, sitemap, crawler access, and citable content. |
| No consistent lead process | Prospect database and outreach preparation. |
| Owner fears a difficult handover | Documented implementation, reporting, and handover evidence. |

Oliviks proof stays out of proposals until the completion checklist is genuinely met and Emmanuel approves the resulting assets.

## Stage 7: Proposal Gate

Before drafting:

1. Confirm the problem in the prospect's words.
2. Select one primary offer.
3. Define the outcome and exclusions.
4. Confirm dependencies and decision-maker.
5. Confirm a realistic timing window.
6. Confirm pricing guidance is current.

Draft with `../templates/SME-DIGITAL-FOUNDATION-PROPOSAL-TEMPLATE-2026.md`.

Before sending:

1. Emmanuel reviews the entire proposal.
2. Every public claim is checked against its source.
3. The currency, tax treatment, payment schedule, and validity date are confirmed.
4. The proposal is exported and visually checked.
5. Sending is approved separately.

## Stage 8: Proposal Follow-Up

Use a prospect-specific follow-up schedule, not an automatic fixed sequence.

- Confirm receipt after the agreed review window.
- Ask what is unclear, not merely whether they decided.
- Record objections in the central content and sales-question queue.
- Close or nurture when there is no active decision path.
- Never count a draft, opened email, or verbal interest as a proposal or closed engagement.

## Required Record After Every Conversation

- Prospect and contact.
- Date and source.
- Problem in the prospect's words.
- Business consequence.
- Decision-maker and decision process.
- Timing.
- Budget signal.
- Recommended offer.
- Relevant proof.
- Objections.
- Agreed next action and date.
- Conversation status: qualified / nurture / no fit.
- Proposal status: not justified / draft approved / sent / won / lost.

HubSpot remains the intended system of record, but any write requires Emmanuel approval.
