## 2. Emmanuel LinkedIn: I changed my mind about llms.txt

**Angle:** Founder judgment and intellectual honesty.

I used to treat `llms.txt` as a core AI-visibility signal.

I still think it can be useful. I no longer describe it as a ranking factor.

The actual specification calls itself a proposal. It creates a concise Markdown overview that an LLM or agent may use at inference time.

That is different from `robots.txt`, which controls crawler access, and `sitemap.xml`, which helps search engines discover URLs.

It is also different from OpenAI's `OAI-SearchBot`, the crawler OpenAI documents for ChatGPT search eligibility.

So my current order of operations is:

1. Make the site accessible and indexable.
2. Make the business identity and services clear.
3. Publish useful, attributable evidence.
4. Add accurate structured data.
5. Keep profiles and reviews consistent.
6. Use `llms.txt` as a low-cost experiment, not a promise.

This is less exciting than claiming one file unlocks ChatGPT.

It is also more useful.

## 4. BridgeWorks LinkedIn: AI visibility is a chain

**Angle:** Framework.

A business can have a fast website and still be difficult for AI-assisted search to use.

AI visibility is a chain:

1. **Access:** can the page load for the relevant crawler?
2. **Discovery:** can the platform find the important URL?
3. **Understanding:** are the business, service, person, and location clear?
4. **Corroboration:** do profiles, reviews, registries, and third-party sources support the claims?
5. **Selection:** is this page the best answer for the specific query?
6. **Conversion:** can the buyer book, call, order, or enquire?

Most audits stop at the first three.

Most businesses need all six.

A citation without a next step is not an acquisition system. A form without discovery is not one either.

## 5. BridgeWorks LinkedIn: ChatGPT search crawler versus training crawler

**Angle:** Practical technical education.

OpenAI documents three different web user agents with different purposes.

- `OAI-SearchBot` supports inclusion in ChatGPT search results.
- `GPTBot` is associated with content that may be used to train foundation models.
- `ChatGPT-User` may visit a page following a user's request.

Allowing search access and allowing model-training access are separate decisions.

A business can choose to allow `OAI-SearchBot` while disallowing `GPTBot` in `robots.txt`.

That distinction is more useful than a generic `allow AI bots` checkbox because it lets the site owner express the actual policy.

It also gives an audit a precise question: is the site eligible for ChatGPT search crawling, and is that choice intentional?

**CTA:** Save this before your next robots.txt review.

**Source:** https://developers.openai.com/api/docs/bots

## 9. Video script: OAI-SearchBot and GPTBot are not the same

**Target length:** 45 seconds

If you run a business website, there is one OpenAI distinction worth knowing.

`OAI-SearchBot` and `GPTBot` are not the same crawler.

OpenAI says OAI-SearchBot is used to surface websites in ChatGPT search results.

GPTBot is used for content that may help train foundation models.

So a site owner can allow search crawling while choosing not to allow training crawling.

The decision lives in `robots.txt`, but hosting and firewall rules also have to allow the published crawler addresses.

This will not guarantee that ChatGPT cites your site. It prevents one avoidable reason the site may be excluded from search crawling.
