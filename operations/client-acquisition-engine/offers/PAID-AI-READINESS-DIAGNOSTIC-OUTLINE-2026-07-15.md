## Why this exists

BridgeWorks should not sell AI automation first.

The safer offer is a paid diagnostic that shows a small business owner where AI can actually help, where process is broken, and where automation would create more mess.

This sits between:

1. **Free public-site observations**: short proof of thinking.

3. **Paid AI Readiness Diagnostic**: owner/process/tool-fit diagnosis before implementation.
4. **Implementation sprint**: only after the business passes readiness checks.

## Positioning

### One-line description

A practical diagnostic for small business owners who want to use AI, but need to know which tasks, tools, and processes are worth fixing first.

### What it is

- A 30 to 45 minute owner interview.
- Transcript-based pain and process extraction.
- Tool/process fit analysis.
- Effort versus impact prioritization.
- A simple quick-start plan.
- A bounded implementation menu.

### What it is not

- Not a promise that AI will save a fixed number of hours.
- Not a generic AI tools list.
- Not a custom automation build.
- Not a software subscription sale.
- Not a guarantee of revenue growth.
- Not a replacement for fixing unclear offers, weak follow-up, or bad operating habits.

## Entry criteria

Run this diagnostic only when at least three are true:

- The business has an identifiable owner or decision-maker.
- There is a repeated task happening weekly.
- The task affects sales, delivery, customer response, reporting, or owner time.
- The owner can give examples, not just vague frustration.
- The business is willing to share screenshots, workflow notes, or tool names.
- There is a realistic path to either process redesign or a small implementation sprint.

## Diagnostic promise

By the end, the owner should know:

1. Which work should be automated.
2. Which work should be simplified before automation.
3. Which AI tools are safe to test now.
4. Which ideas should be rejected for now.
5. What a small implementation sprint would include if they continue.

## Interview questions

### Owner day

1. Walk me through a normal working day from first customer/task to close.
2. Which task do you avoid even though it matters?
3. What work piles up when the week gets busy?
4. What do customers, leads, or staff wait on because you are the bottleneck?
5. What do you copy, paste, rewrite, chase, or check repeatedly?

### Sales and lead flow

6. Where do new enquiries come from now?
7. What happens in the first 10 minutes after a lead arrives?
8. What leads go cold because response or follow-up is slow?
9. Which questions do prospects ask again and again?
10. Where do quotes, proposals, bookings, or follow-ups get stuck?

### Delivery and operations

11. Which part of delivery has too many manual steps?
12. What information do you ask for repeatedly from clients or staff?
13. What mistakes happen because instructions live in someone’s head?
14. What reports, updates, reminders, or checks happen every week?
15. What process would you delete if you could?

### Tools and previous attempts

16. What tools do you already use?
17. What automations or AI tools have you tried before?
18. What failed, and why?
19. What are you paying for that does not save time?
20. What tool would you not want to change right now?

### Capacity and budget reality

21. Who will maintain the system after setup?
22. How much time can the owner or team give to setup in the first week?
23. What is the monthly cost ceiling for tools?
24. What would make this worth paying for?
25. What would make this a bad idea right now?

## Analysis framework

Score each candidate improvement from 1 to 5.

| Dimension | 1 | 3 | 5 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pain | Annoying only | Costs time weekly | Blocks sales, delivery, or owner focus |
| Frequency | Monthly | Weekly | Daily or every lead/client |
| Process clarity | Unclear | Partly mapped | Clear repeated path |
| Tool fit | Custom build needed | Existing tool may work | Simple existing tool or workflow fits |
| Owner capacity | No time to maintain | Can review weekly | Can operate or delegate clearly |
| ROI clarity | Hard to measure | Time saved likely | Revenue, response, or cost effect visible |
| Risk | Sensitive or fragile | Manageable | Low-risk internal workflow |

## Output report structure

### 1. Executive summary

- Best AI opportunity.
- Best non-AI process fix.
- Biggest risk if they automate too early.
- Recommended next action.

### 2. Current bottleneck map

| Bottleneck | Evidence from interview | Business impact | Current owner workaround |
|---|---|---|---|

### 3. Process-before-automation findings

For each process:

- Current step count.
- Step count after simplification.
- What should be removed.
- What should be standardized.
- What can later be automated.

### 4. Effort versus impact matrix

| Recommendation | Impact | Effort | Risk | Tool/process type | Owner role |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

### 5. Recommended quick wins

Use only 3 to 5 recommendations.

Each one must include:

- Problem.
- Recommended fix.
- Why now.
- Tool or process needed.
- Setup effort.
- Owner maintenance burden.
- Proof of success.

### 6. 4-day quick-start plan

| Day | Focus | Action | Proof |
|---|---|---|---|
| Day 1 | Map | Document one repeated workflow and remove unnecessary steps. | Clean current-state map. |
| Day 2 | Standardize | Create the message, checklist, form, or template that makes the process repeatable. | Reusable asset exists. |
| Day 3 | Tool test | Test one low-cost/no-cost tool or AI-assisted workflow on real but safe data. | Test output reviewed manually. |
| Day 4 | Decision | Decide: keep manual, improve process, or build small implementation sprint. | Clear next-step decision. |

### 7. Financial impact

Avoid imported promises.

Use simple estimates:

- Hours saved per week.
- Owner hourly value estimate.
- Lead response improvement potential.
- Tool subscription cost.
- Setup cost.
- Maintenance time.
- Risk if wrong tool is adopted.

Formula:

```text
Monthly value estimate = weekly hours saved × owner hourly value × 4
Net monthly value = monthly value estimate - monthly tool cost - monthly maintenance cost
```

If the number is weak, say so.

### 8. Implementation upsell menu

Only offer after the diagnostic.

| Sprint | Use when | Includes | Excludes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Process Redesign Sprint | Workflow is messy before AI. | Workflow map, simplified steps, templates, SOP. | Custom automation, new CRM migration, client-facing promises. |
| AI Tool Setup Sprint | Existing tool fits the process. | Tool setup, prompts/templates, test workflow, handover note. | Paid subscriptions unless approved, complex integrations. |
| Lead Response Sprint | Leads are leaking through slow response or poor follow-up. | Enquiry route, response draft system, follow-up checklist, measurement. | Sending outreach, WhatsApp blasting, unsupported claims. |
| Reporting/Content Assist Sprint | Owner repeats reporting or content preparation. | Reusable reporting/content workflow, source folder rules, QA checklist. | Autopublishing, fake proof, unsupervised external output. |

## BridgeWorks internal decision rules

### Recommend process redesign when

- The steps are unclear.
- The owner cannot explain the workflow twice the same way.
- The work depends on memory or WhatsApp scattered notes.
- Automation would only make confusion faster.

### Recommend off-the-shelf tools when

- The process is simple and repeated.
- A common tool can solve 80 percent of the need.
- Setup risk is low.
- The owner can maintain it.

### Recommend custom implementation when

- The process is stable.
- Existing tools do not fit.
- The expected value is higher than build and maintenance cost.
- The client can approve scope, data access, and ongoing ownership.

### Reject or pause when

- The owner wants AI to replace sales clarity.
- The workflow touches high-risk personal, financial, legal, or medical data.
- There is no owner capacity for maintenance.
- The business cannot define success.
- The request depends on unapproved outreach, scraping, publishing, or paid tools.

### Boundary line
