# BridgeWorks Content 90-Day Rollout (Proposed)

Version: 1.0 (proposed)
Created: 2026-07-22
Owner: Emmanuel Ehigbai
Status: Proposed plan. No schedule is created and nothing is published by this file. Pairs with `content-channel-model.md` and `cross-model-content-cycle-runbook.md`.

Use three weekly master theses. Adapt each across channels. Do not create unrelated daily ideas for every platform.

## Days 1 to 30

- Personal LinkedIn
- BridgeWorks LinkedIn
- Personal Instagram
- BridgeWorks Instagram
- Personal Facebook
- BridgeWorks Facebook
- Manual Nigerian WhatsApp Status
- Hungarian WhatsApp Business preparation (checklist and templates only)
- No TikTok publishing
- No full X cadence

## Days 31 to 60

- Continue the core channels
- Add X if the first month is operationally stable
- Double down on winning hooks, formats, and service topics
- Test at least one founder-led vertical video each week

## Days 61 to 90

- Add TikTok only if two vertical videos per week are sustainable
- Run a conversion-focused service campaign
- Produce a 90-day learning report

## Suggested service sequence

1. Strategy and transformation
2. Digital platforms and brand systems
3. Websites and conversion paths
4. Content, visibility, and demand
5. AI and workflow automation
6. Execution and operating systems
7. Africa-Europe market entry where evidence supports the claim

## Content mix

- 35 percent pain and diagnosis
- 30 percent simplified expertise
- 20 percent proof, process, and build evidence
- 10 percent founder perspective
- 5 percent direct conversion

## Service explainer structure

Every service explainer uses this sequence:

Pain -> diagnostic insight -> applicability -> intervention -> outcome -> proof or boundary -> next step

The video or carousel carries the argument. The caption adds nuance, examples, proof, and links.

## Voice rules

- No em dashes. No AI slop.
- Avoid: world-class, innovative, seamless, ecosystem, game-changing, transformative, robust, cutting-edge, passionate about, leverage, synergize.
- Short sentences. One main idea per sentence. Specific over general. Real evidence only.
- Never invent metrics, clients, outcomes, testimonials, prices, or partnerships.
- High expertise, simple language, global-market framing.
- Do not limit BridgeWorks to SMEs or one geography unless the brief explicitly calls for it.
