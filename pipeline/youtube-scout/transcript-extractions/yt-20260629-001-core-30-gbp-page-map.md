## Core extraction

For local service businesses, the website should support the Google Business Profile instead of floating separately from it.

The usable pattern is a GBP-to-website map:

1. Treat the Google Business Profile as the local SEO money page.
2. Match website hierarchy to GBP primary category, secondary categories, and services.
3. Use the homepage or GBP landing page for the primary category plus city.
4. Create supporting category and service pages only when scope and business reality justify them.
5. Use internal links to mirror the service hierarchy.
6. Make the first paragraph answer the buyer's search intent, not company history.
7. Add hyperlocal details only when they are real and specific.

## BridgeWorks adaptation

Add a `Core 30 GBP Page Map` check to Foundation QA. Keep it proportional.

For a Foundation site, do not build 30 pages by default. Use it as a diagnostic and expansion map:

- Primary GBP category -> homepage or main service section.
- Secondary categories -> homepage sections or service pages if scope allows.
- High-value services -> sections first, pages later if there is search demand.
- Locations -> real location page only when the business has a real location or service-area need.
- First paragraph -> buyer intent, service, location, reason to call.
- Reviews and photos -> real client evidence only.
