## Core Thesis

A beginner can win AI-service work without testimonials by targeting urgent buyer intent, translating past experience into the buyer's problem, showing a small proof asset, and using a short Loom to diagnose the job.

## Problem

New service sellers get stuck because clients want proof, but proof usually requires clients. Sandy frames it as the testimonial loop.

Evidence:

- `[00:10] I didn't have any testimonials, no case study, and no portfolio.`
- `[00:20] their organic keyword research grew by 1,000% by March this year.`
- `[01:27] my first client? It's actually through Upwork.`
- `[10:14] I sent a quick Loom video... showcasing my portfolio.`
- `[11:09] go to Claude and ask Claude, what do you think their problem is.`

## Workflow

1. Pick a high-intent source.
2. Capture the buyer's stated problem.
3. Ask Claude to infer the real business problem.
4. Build a tiny proof asset tied to that problem.
5. Record a short Loom or create a one-page audit preview.
6. Ask for a call or a narrow next step.
7. Convert the first result into a case study.

## Business Model

This supports a BridgeWorks "first proof sprint" model. Small fixed-scope audit preview, then Foundation website, SEO/GEO setup, or automation implementation.

## Service Fulfillment Lessons

- A portfolio can be self-made proof. It does not need to be a client testimonial.
- The asset must match the buyer's stated pain.
- Use AI to interpret the job or website, but keep the message human and specific.
- Do not claim outcomes before proof exists.
