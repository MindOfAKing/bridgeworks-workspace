## Core idea
The useful shift is from one-shot prompts to bounded loops. A loop watches, remembers, filters, drafts, and stops at a human gate.

## Evidence
- 02:21 to 02:32: a good loop answers five questions: what to watch, how often to check, what change matters, what to do, and when to stop and ask.
- 07:49 to 08:28: demand-signal loop scans Reddit, X, funding announcements, hiring pages, grades signals, drafts a message, and never sends without approval.
- 09:13 to 09:30: the loop should report where sources failed instead of pretending the run was perfect.
- 11:34 to 12:10: money, reputation, customers, and legal actions require a human gate.

## BridgeWorks fit
High. This matches BridgeWorks' need for a simple prospect-intelligence routine without outreach automation. The right adaptation is a draft-only demand-signal scout for local SME pain, not auto-contact.

## Adoption rule
Adopt only with hard gates:
1. Watch sources only.
2. Score signals.
3. Draft possible next steps.
4. Never send messages.
5. Log source failures.
6. Ask Emmanuel before outreach, publishing, spend, or client contact.
