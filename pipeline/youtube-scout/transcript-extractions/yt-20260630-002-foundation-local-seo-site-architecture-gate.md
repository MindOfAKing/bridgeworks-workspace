## Core Thesis

AI-built local websites fail when they look polished but lack search structure. The useful BridgeWorks lesson is to make site architecture, service/location pages, local proof, schema, mobile QA, speed, and staging checks part of Foundation website production before visual polish.

## Frameworks

| Framework | Adopt / Adapt / Reject | BridgeWorks Use |
|---|---|---|
| Service and location silo | Adapt | Use proportionally. Do not default every Foundation client into a large page build. |
| Local area research before page copy | Adopt | Require real verifiable details for location pages. |
| One-page template, then replicate | Adopt | Get homepage, service page, and one location page right before scaling. |
| Staging and SEO QA before launch | Adopt | Add to Foundation QA. |

## Systems And Processes

- 00:23 to 00:58: start with research and site structure, not design.
- 01:49 to 02:19: create service parent pages and dedicated service pages, plus area parent pages and dedicated area pages.
- 03:01 to 03:18: collect business type, brand name, locations, and services before asking Claude Code to build.
- 06:44 to 07:03: show the site structure to the client before moving further so missing services or areas surface early.
- 12:12 to 13:08: homepage must make the offer and next action clear within 2 seconds, with trust, services, reviews, map, and FAQ.
- 15:48 to 16:24: create an image style guide so service images look coherent across the site.
- 22:42 to 23:05: use real verifiable details and say when unsure rather than inventing facts.
- 35:24 to 36:14: use contextual source links without turning transactional pages into research articles.
- 37:03 to 38:23: check H1, CTA, local proof, reviews, map, title tag, meta description, headings, LocalBusiness/service schema, and FAQ schema.
- 38:43 to 39:20: publish to staging first and check speed before launch.

## Service Fulfillment Lessons

BridgeWorks Foundation sites need an architecture gate before design. For Oliviks and future SMBs, this means exact services, exact areas served, first CTA, trust proof, menu or service clarity, local relevance, schema, mobile QA, and speed.

## Sales / Positioning Angles
