## Why it matters

The transcript is short, but the idea is useful for BridgeWorks audits. Many service-business symptoms point upstream to offer, pricing power, or sales motion. BridgeWorks should diagnose the root bottleneck before prescribing website, automation, or content work.

## BridgeWorks adaptation

Add a `Root Bottleneck Check` to audit previews and offer calls:

1. Acquisition: not enough qualified customers.
2. Conversion: enough attention, weak trust or sales path.
3. Retention: customers leave or do not repeat.
4. Value capture: not enough revenue per customer.
5. Delivery capacity: owner is trapped because price and process cannot fund support.
6. Visibility: good offer exists, but market cannot find or understand it.

A website, automation, or GEO fix should be tied to the actual bottleneck. If the bottleneck is price or offer clarity, do not pretend a prettier website alone solves it.

## Reject or guardrail

- Do not use this to push a larger package.
- Do not diagnose from one symptom only.
- Do not assume every local SME needs automation first.
- Do not promise premium pricing without proof, positioning, and delivery capacity.
