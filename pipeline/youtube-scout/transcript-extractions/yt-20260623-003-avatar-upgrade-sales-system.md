## Source-grounded evidence

- `[00:00] When you first start growing, you'll take almost whatever client you can get.`
- `[00:05] it takes the same amount of work to sell one person to do everything as it would to sell a client that wants to do a single process.`
- `[00:21] you just have to be comfortable with the fact that like you have to say no to people.`
- `[00:36] it has to change from the top all the way down.`
- `[00:39] the messaging in the funnel, the case studies you show, the lead magnets you have, the sales scripting... Everything has to change to match the new avatar.`
- `[00:58] same sales velocity where your LTV will be 5 to 10 times higher.`

## Practical framework

Before BridgeWorks tries to sell higher-value work, update the whole sales chain:

1. Define the new buyer type.
2. Remove lead magnets that attract low-fit work.
3. Rewrite homepage and offer copy for the new avatar.
4. Select case studies that match the new buyer.
5. Rewrite sales questions and disqualification rules.
6. Practice saying no to lower-fit work.

## BridgeWorks fit

Medium-high. BridgeWorks is still early and should not pretend to serve large teams. But the guardrail matters: do not sell complex one-off requests to anyone who asks. Keep Foundation offer simple and move toward higher-LTV clients through qualification, not scope creep.

## Next experiment

Add a one-page `Foundation Client Fit Filter`:

- Need a simple site and visibility foundation.
- Has assets or can provide them quickly.
- Accepts static-first scope.
- Has budget and decision authority.
- Does not require custom app, full ecommerce, or ongoing agency support unless separately scoped.
