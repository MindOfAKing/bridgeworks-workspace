## Core extraction

Local AI visibility should not become a fear-based GEO product. The practical stack is still:

1. Google Business Profile clarity.
2. Website pages that support the service and location claims.
3. Targeted local content.
4. Reviews with service/location context.
5. Backlinks and local authority.
6. A short answer/elevator-pitch block that AI systems can quote.

## BridgeWorks adaptation

Add a `Local SEO And AI Search Signal Stack` to Foundation QA:

- GBP description says who, what, where, and proof.
- Homepage has a direct service/location answer block.
- Core services have separate sections or pages when scope allows.
- Location/service claims match GBP.
- Reviews are requested ethically and tied to real outcomes.
- Local citations and backlinks are logged as authority work.
- DGE reporting separates Maps/Search basics from AI visibility experiments.

## Guardrails

- No AI-ranking guarantees.
- No fake reviews.
- No paid citation/backlink campaigns without approval.
- No schema-only or GEO-only pitch when GBP, website hierarchy, reviews, and content are weak.
