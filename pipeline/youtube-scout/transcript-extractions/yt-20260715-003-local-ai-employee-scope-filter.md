BridgeWorks pillar: AI Growth Systems, Foundation Offer, Sales And Prospecting

## Useful Ideas

- Local SMEs still need basic lead infrastructure: website, chat, missed-call text-back, booking, review request flow, follow-up, inbox, and CRM.
- Prospecting by missing website can identify need, but the pitch style must stay approval-gated and truthful.
- The strongest BridgeWorks use is not cloning the affiliate model. It is turning the stack into a lead-leak checklist.
- The `free website first` tactic is risky for BridgeWorks because it can blur scope and create unpaid delivery before qualification.

## Evidence

- 01:49 to 02:29: target small local businesses without websites and offer a free website to start the conversation.
- 03:37 to 04:50: the prospecting tool scans Google Maps, filters no-website businesses, captures contact data, and builds a basic demo site.
- 06:04 to 07:17: the proposed upgrade stack includes website, AI chat widget, voice receptionist, missed-call text-back, appointment calendar, review automation, lead pipeline, follow-up sequences, and unified inbox.
- 07:36 to 08:04: missed-call text-back is positioned as a safer alternative when owners do not want voice AI.
- 08:08 to 08:35: review automation is framed as asking real customers to review, not fake reviews.
- 09:01 to 09:22: unified inbox prevents missed messages across email, text, Facebook, Instagram, and website chat.

## What This Changes For BridgeWorks

Use the stack as a diagnostic checklist for Lead Leak Review. Do not copy the free-website outreach model. BridgeWorks is already trying to land qualified retainers and finish Oliviks cleanly. Free speculative websites can become scope creep.
