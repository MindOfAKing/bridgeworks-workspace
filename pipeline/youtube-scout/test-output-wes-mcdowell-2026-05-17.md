## Core Thesis

A homepage should not be a generic introduction. It should be a sequenced conversion path that helps a visitor understand the problem, trust the business, see the solution, and take the next step.

## Frameworks

| Framework | Adopt / Adapt / Reject | BridgeWorks Use |
|---|---|---|
| Homepage as ordered conversion path | Adopt | Use in website audits and website builds. |
| Hero section must quickly clarify what the business does and why it matters | Adopt | Add to conversion-audit-preview reports. |
| Pain-point section pulls visitors deeper into the page | Adapt | Use carefully for professional CEE service businesses. Keep it practical, not dramatic. |
| Short founder/business video can increase trust | Adapt | Recommend only where the owner can record a credible 60 to 90 second video. |
| Do not copy competitor homepage structure blindly | Adopt | Use as a market-audit insight for prospects with generic websites. |

## Systems And Processes

BridgeWorks should adapt this into a **Service Business Homepage Audit Checklist**:

1. Does the hero say who this is for?
2. Does the hero say the outcome?
3. Is the CTA specific?
4. Does the page name the buyer’s problem?
5. Is there credible proof?
6. Is there a clear next step?
7. Is the contact path simple?
8. Does the page answer trust objections before the form?

## Service Fulfillment Lessons

For Digital Infrastructure:

- Website builds should start from conversion path, not page decoration.
- Every service business homepage should be treated as a sales conversation.
- Proof, process, service clarity, and next step matter more than visual polish alone.

For Content & Visibility:

- YouTube or educational content can pre-sell expertise before a discovery call.
- BridgeWorks can later adapt this into authority content for founders and service businesses.
