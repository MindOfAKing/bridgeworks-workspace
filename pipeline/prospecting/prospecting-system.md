# BridgeWorks Prospecting System

Version: 1.0  
Owner: Emmanuel Ehigbai  
Primary campaign: CEE property-service businesses  
Created: 2026-05-17

## Purpose

This system exists to create qualified conversations every week.

Inbound protects demand. Prospecting creates demand.

The first proof-adjacent target is property-service businesses in Budapest and Central Europe because BridgeWorks already has relevant proof from CEEFM.

This is not a Hungary-only system. BridgeWorks should prospect wherever the pain is visible, the buyer can pay, and the offer can be tied to a measurable business outcome.

Primary market lanes:

- CEE property-service businesses: Hungary, Czech Republic, Poland, Austria, Romania, Slovakia, Croatia.
- UK and EU serviced apartment, property management, FM, cleaning, and hospitality operators.
- Nigeria and wider Africa facility management, commercial cleaning, property care, hospitality, and real-estate service companies.
- Other English-speaking service businesses where speed-to-lead, website conversion, GEO, or workflow automation pain is obvious.

## Weekly Output Target

Every week:

- 100 raw companies found
- 50 companies qualified
- 25 personalized first messages drafted
- 25 first messages sent
- 50 follow-ups scheduled
- 5 discovery conversations started
- 1 proposal opportunity created

## Daily Output Target

Every prospecting day:

- Find 20 companies

Do not end a prospecting block with leads sitting in "New". Every lead needs a next action date.

## Market Triage Before Prospecting

Before starting a 7-day sprint, run the market layer.

Use:

- `agent-market-strategy` to judge market value, offer fit, and revenue opportunity.
- `agent-market-competitive` to identify positioning gaps and market language.
- `agent-market-conversion` on sample prospect websites to find conversion leaks.
- `agent-market-technical` on sample prospect websites when SEO, tracking, schema, or AI visibility is likely to matter.

Do not prospect a niche just because it has many companies.

Prospect it if:

- The pain is visible.
- The buyer can afford BridgeWorks.
- The outreach angle is specific.
- The offer can be explained in one sentence.
- A case study from the niche would help BridgeWorks sell more work.

## Core Offer

Lead with one sharp offer:

## Free Audit Promise

BridgeWorks can offer a free audit without giving away the full implementation plan.

The free audit should be a **preview report**, not the entire diagnosis.

It should prove:

- BridgeWorks looked at their actual business.
- The issue is specific.
- The issue likely affects revenue, trust, or lead quality.
- A conversation would be useful.

It should not give away:

- Step-by-step implementation instructions.
- Full keyword map.
- Full schema plan.
- Full competitor teardown.
- Full automation workflow.
- Exact copy rewrite for every page.
- Full technical remediation list.

### Free Audit Report Size

Use a 2 to 4 page branded PDF.

Sections:

1. Snapshot
2. Three findings
3. Business impact
4. Recommended next step

### Report Types

Use the report type that matches the outreach angle:

- `geo-audit-preview` for AI visibility, search, schema, and citability.
- `market-audit-preview` for positioning, trust, offer, and competitor gaps.
- `conversion-audit-preview` for website lead path, CTA, proof, and friction.
- `technical-audit-preview` for SEO foundation, tracking, site structure, performance, and metadata.
- `speed-to-lead-preview` for response path, form flow, routing, and follow-up.

### What To Include

Include:

- Company name
- Website reviewed
- Date reviewed
- The visible signal
- Three prioritized findings
- One quick win
- One bigger opportunity
- BridgeWorks recommended next step

### What To Hold Back

Hold back:

- Full execution checklist
- Exact implementation sequence
- Tool configuration
- Full competitor matrix
- Full prompt/workflow logic
- Full page-by-page rewrite

The line is simple:

Show the leak. Do not hand over the plumbing diagram.

## Qualification Signals

Strong prospect if at least three are true:

- Business sells high-value recurring service
- Website exists but looks underpowered
- No clear lead capture path
- Contact form exists but looks slow or generic
- Google Business Profile has reviews but weak website conversion
- Founder or team is visible on LinkedIn
- Serves B2B, property, hospitality, or residential complexes
- Operates in English, Hungarian, German, Romanian, or Nigerian market English
- Likely deal value can support EUR 500 audit or EUR 1,500+ system build

Weak prospect if:

- No visible business activity
- No website and no clear public footprint
- Pure micro-business with no obvious budget
- Franchise branch with no local buying authority
- Only wants cheap web design
- No clear revenue problem BridgeWorks can affect

## Daily Workflow

### 1. Build Raw List

Search one niche and one location.

Example:

```text
facility management Budapest
property management Budapest
cleaning company Budapest offices
aparthotel operator Budapest
serviced apartments Budapest management
```

Add raw companies to the tracker.

### 2. Qualify

For each company, check:

- Website
- Google Business Profile
- LinkedIn
- Contact page
- Lead capture path
- Visible trust signals
- Business model fit

Write one specific signal. Not a vague note.

Bad:

```text
Website could be better.
```

Good:

```text
Website has service pages but no clear quote path, response promise, or proof from managed properties.
```

### 3. Choose Outreach Angle

Use one of four angles:

1. Website conversion leak
2. Speed-to-lead risk
3. Weak proof and trust signals
4. AI/Google visibility gap

### 4. Draft Message

Draft one short message tied to the visible signal.

Do not sound like a newsletter.

Do not open with a compliment unless it is specific.

Every draft must include a clear pain versus outcome contrast:

- Pain: the visible problem, friction, missed opportunity, or risk the prospect is likely feeling.
- Outcome: the practical business result BridgeWorks can help create.

If the message only names the problem but does not name the outcome, rewrite it before sending.

### 5. Send Or Queue

Send A priority prospects the same day.

Queue B priority prospects for the next prospecting block.

### 6. Schedule Follow-Ups

Every contacted prospect gets:

- Follow-up 1 after 3 business days
- Follow-up 2 after 7 business days
- Dormant move after 14 days if no reply

```text
The outcome I would look for is a clearer path from website visit to qualified conversation.

I run BridgeWorks. We help service businesses make that path sharper.

Worth sending you a 3-point teardown?

Emmanuel
BridgeWorks.agency
```

### Speed-To-Lead

```text
Hi [Name],

Quick note. I was looking at [Company] and noticed your enquiry path depends on [form/email/phone].

For property-service companies, speed-to-lead is often the quiet leak. A good enquiry goes cold because the first response is too slow or too generic.

That is the pain: a real enquiry can disappear before anyone has a proper sales conversation.

The outcome I would look for is a response path that makes every new lead visible, fast to handle, and easy to follow up.

BridgeWorks builds simple response and follow-up systems for this.

Should I send you a short example of what that could look like?

Emmanuel
BridgeWorks.agency
```

### Proof Gap

```text

I found [Company] while looking at property-service firms in [City].

You seem to offer [specific service], but the website does not make the proof easy to scan: property types, response process, client examples, and why someone should trust you before calling.

That is the pain: the buyer may need you, but the proof does not reduce enough doubt before they contact you.

The outcome I would look for is a page that makes trust obvious in under a minute.

I can send a short teardown if useful.

Emmanuel
BridgeWorks.agency
```

```text
Emmanuel
BridgeWorks.agency
```

## Operating Rules

1. One campaign at a time.
2. One tracker as source of truth.
3. One next action per prospect.
4. No prospect stays in "Message drafted" for more than 24 hours.
5. No discounting the audit. Reduce scope if needed.
6. No new prospecting tool until the current tracker is being used daily.
7. Every outreach draft, follow-up draft, and audit preview must include pain versus outcome before it is sent.

### Day 1

Create tracker. Build first 50 raw leads.

### Day 2

Qualify first 25. Draft first 10 messages.

### Day 3

Send 10 messages. Schedule follow-ups.

### Day 4

Build 25 more raw leads. Qualify 15.

### Day 5

Send 10 more messages. Follow up on early replies.

### Day 6

Review signals. Improve message templates.

### Day 7

Score results. Pick next niche or continue same niche.

## Success Metrics

Track weekly:

- Raw prospects added
- Qualified prospects
- Messages sent
- Replies
- Positive replies
- Discovery calls booked
- Proposals created
- Wins

If there are no replies after 50 messages, fix the niche, signal quality, or first line.

If there are replies but no calls, fix the offer.

If there are calls but no proposals, fix qualification.
