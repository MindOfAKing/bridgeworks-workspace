Ops Platform Build Options

An overview of what it takes to build a dedicated operations platform for managing tickets, properties, and maintenance across multiple client sites.

Prepared by BridgeWorks · 17 April 2026

Summary

This document outlines three options for building a dedicated operations platform. The platform would handle ticket management, property tracking, maintenance scheduling, and reporting across multiple client sites. Each tier is described with its scope, timeline, and cost. Questions for Victor are listed at the end.

Three build options

Each tier builds on the previous one.

Tier 1
Clickable Prototype
EUR 1,200 one-time
2 to 3 weeks · No hosting cost

A static demo with fake data. Looks and feels real. No database, no login. Hosted as a gated demo URL on your domain.

  • 6 interactive screens
  • Your company branding throughout
  • Ticket list with status badges
  • Create ticket modal
  • Filter panel with all fields
  • Properties and units grid
  • Guest reviews table
  • Notifications list
  • Desktop + tablet + mobile layouts
  • Password-protected access
Tier 2
MVP Working App
EUR 4,500 one-time
6 to 8 weeks · EUR 50 to 100/mo hosting

A real application with a database. Staff log in, create tickets, attach photos, and track progress across all properties.

  • Everything in Tier 1 (real, not demo)
  • User authentication (email + password)
  • Create, edit, assign, close tickets
  • Photo and file attachments
  • Property and unit management
  • Status workflow (Todo, In Progress, Awaiting Reply, Done)
  • Search and filter across all fields
  • EN + HU language support
  • Admin, Manager, Staff roles
Tier 3
Production Platform
EUR 12,000 one-time
12 to 16 weeks · EUR 100 to 200/mo hosting

Full production platform. Roles, notifications, reporting, mobile cleaner view, and a read-only client portal.

  • Everything in Tier 2
  • Guest review import and tracking
  • Email and SMS notifications
  • 5 roles: Admin, City Manager, Supervisor, Maintenance, Cleaner
  • Mobile cleaner view (checklist-style)
  • CSV and PDF report export
  • Full audit log
  • Client portal (read-only access per client)
  • Help centre with cleaning manuals
  • White-label ready for licensing

Questions for Victor

We need your answers to scope this correctly. Please respond with as much detail as you can.

QUESTION 01

How many properties do you manage today, and how many do you expect in 12 months?

Determines whether the system needs multi-city support from day one and how the property list should be structured.

QUESTION 02

How many staff would need accounts?

Affects the hosting tier and whether role-based access is needed from the start or can be added later.

QUESTION 03

What ticket types do you use beyond Repair and Maintenance?

Examples: Cleaning, Inspection, Handover, Deep Clean, Pest Control. The system is built to match your actual workflow.

QUESTION 04

Do your clients currently receive any service reports? If yes, in what format?

If clients already receive PDFs or spreadsheets, the platform can generate those automatically instead.

QUESTION 05

Should the platform carry your company brand, or would you prefer a separate product name?

A separate name is useful if you plan to license the platform to other companies later. Your own brand is simpler if it stays internal.

QUESTION 06

What is the budget range you have in mind?

Helps us prioritise the right tier and features within your range.

QUESTION 07

Is there a deadline or event driving the timeline?

A new client onboarding, contract renewal, or internal milestone would change the build priority.

QUESTION 08

Should the platform support Hungarian-language UI, or is English sufficient?

Bilingual UI adds roughly 20% to build time. If only internal staff use it and they work in English, a single language saves time.
