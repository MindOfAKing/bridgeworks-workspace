### Post 5 - Wednesday, April 9 | Industry Insight

**Topic:** Energy costs in Budapest buildings and what FM can do about it

**English version:**

Energy costs for Budapest commercial buildings rose 40% between 2022 and 2025.

Most property managers absorbed the increase. Few optimized.

Here is what we see working across our managed properties:

LED lighting retrofits. Payback period: 8-14 months. After that, pure savings.

Smart HVAC scheduling. Matching heating and cooling to actual occupancy instead of fixed schedules cuts energy use by 15-20%.

Building envelope audits. Finding and fixing air leaks is the cheapest energy upgrade available. Most buildings have them. Few check.

Motion-sensor lighting in common areas. Stairwells, parking garages, storage rooms. Lights that run 24/7 when nobody is there are burning money.

Regular boiler and chiller maintenance. A 5% efficiency drop from neglected maintenance compounds fast across a full building.

None of this requires major capital investment. It requires attention.

**Topic:** Smart building technology. What actually delivers ROI.

**English version:**

Every facility management conference in 2026 talks about smart buildings.

Most of the technology being sold has a payback period longer than the contract term. Here is what actually works for mid-size properties in Budapest:

Smart thermostats with occupancy sensing. Cost: 200-400 EUR per unit. Savings: 10-18% on heating and cooling. Payback: under 2 years. This is the clearest win.

Water leak sensors. Cost: 50-100 EUR per sensor. A single undetected leak can cause 10,000+ EUR in damage. The math is obvious.

Digital maintenance ticketing. Cost: 30-80 EUR per month for a property. Benefit: traceable response times, data on recurring issues, accountability. Not a direct cost saving but it prevents expensive problems.

IoT-connected lighting in common areas. Cost: moderate. Savings: real but slower payback (3-4 years). Worth it for larger properties.

Digital twins and AI-driven predictive maintenance? Promising for large portfolios. Overkill for a 50-unit apartment building.

Start with what pays back fastest. Scale from there.
