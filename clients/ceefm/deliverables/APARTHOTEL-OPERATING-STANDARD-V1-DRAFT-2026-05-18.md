# Aparthotel Operating Standard v1

Client: CEEFM Kft  
Status: Internal draft for Week 8  
Use: LinkedIn content, funnel page proof, sales conversation asset, future downloadable guide

## Positioning

Aparthotel operations sit between hotel housekeeping and residential facility management.

They need hotel-level guest readiness, residential-style building care, and fast maintenance escalation without relying on a full front-desk team.

CEEFM's operating standard should communicate one idea:

> Clean rooms are only one part of aparthotel performance. The full guest path has to work.

## 14-Point Aparthotel Operating Standard

### 1. Arrival Path Readiness

The guest experience starts before the room.

Check:

- Building entrance
- Door access area
- Lift and stairwell route
- Corridor scent, lighting, and visible marks
- First impression from street to room door

### 2. Room Turnover Checklist

Every room turnover should follow a visible checklist, not memory.

Minimum checks:

- Bed presentation
- Floor and visible surface cleanliness
- Bathroom readiness
- Kitchen area readiness
- Waste removal
- Window, curtain, and odor check

### 3. Bathroom Hygiene Protocol

Bathrooms drive guest trust quickly.

Check:

- Sink, taps, mirror, shower, toilet
- Drain smell
- Hair and residue
- Refill items
- Ventilation signs

### 4. Kitchen And Appliance Inspection

Aparthotel kitchens create more guest friction than standard hotel rooms.

Check:

- Hob and extractor
- Fridge interior
- Microwave or oven
- Kettle and coffee area
- Sink and drain
- Utensils and visible marks

### 5. Linen And Consumables Control

Turnover speed depends on stock discipline.

Check:

- Linen availability
- Towel stock
- Consumables refill
- Damaged or missing items
- Escalation route for low inventory

### 6. Common Area Walkthrough

Common areas affect every guest, not just one booking.

Check:

- Lobby or entrance area
- Hallways
- Lifts
- Stairwells
- Shared bins
- Signage and floor-level issues

### 7. Maintenance Escalation Rules

Cleaning teams often see problems before guests report them.

Escalate:

- Broken fixtures
- Water leaks
- Lighting faults
- Door or access issues
- Appliance issues
- Safety risks

### 8. Digital Ticket Discipline

Every issue needs a status.

Suggested statuses:

- Received
- Scheduled
- In progress
- Resolved
- Waiting on supplier

### 9. Supervisor Quality Checks

Supervision protects consistency.

Use supervisor review for:

- Random room checks
- First-room-of-day checks
- New staff training
- Complaint-linked room checks
- Weekly pattern review

### 10. Guest Complaint Pattern Review

Complaints should become system improvements.

Track:

- Repeated room issues
- Repeated appliance issues
- Repeated common-area issues
- Repeated timing failures
- Repeated supplier delays

### 11. Weekly Preventive Walkthrough

Preventive walkthroughs reduce urgent repairs.

Check:

- Lighting
- Hallway cleanliness
- Lift presentation
- Door function
- Water signs
- Noise or vibration indicators

### 12. Operations Summary

Operators should know what happened without chasing.

Weekly summary should include:

- Completed cleaning volume
- Issues found
- Issues resolved
- Open maintenance items
- Guest-impact risks
- Next-week priorities

### 13. Seasonal Readiness

Budapest properties need seasonal preparation.

Examples:

- Summer ventilation and odor control
- Winter entrance matting and floor safety
- Holiday turnover peaks
- Outdoor area readiness
- Heating/cooling issue monitoring

### 14. Account Review Rhythm

The system improves only if reviewed.

Monthly review should cover:

- Service quality
- Complaint patterns
- Response time
- Staffing pressure
- Guest-rating movement
- Next improvement actions
