**What these technical bits mean for you:**

- **Meta descriptions** are the one or two lines of text that show under your business name on Google search results. We rewrite them so people clicking from search arrive ready to order.
- **Open Graph tags** control what shows up when oliviks.com gets shared on Facebook, LinkedIn, or WhatsApp. Right now your shared links may look broken or generic. After this work they show your logo, a clear headline, and a real photo.
- **Alt text** is a hidden text description on every image. It helps screen readers for visually impaired customers and lets your food photos appear in Google image searches.
- **Page speed and caching** make your pages load faster. Slower sites lose mobile customers and rank lower on Google. We tune your site to load fast on a phone.
- **301 redirects** automatically forward people who land on old or broken URLs to the right page. So if someone hits /cart-2, they end up on /cart instead of an error.
- **Mobile responsive QA** is the manual test of every page on a phone-size screen. Most of your customers order from their phone. We make sure none of them see a broken layout.

| 12 | One-page handover document: how to add a post, respond to a review, upload a photo. |

**What these technical bits mean for you:**

- **Secondary categories** are extra labels Google uses to file your business in more search categories. Adding "Nigerian Restaurant", "Caterer", and "Takeaway" means you show up when people search any of those, not just "African restaurant".
- **Attributes** are the checkboxes Google offers for filtering search results: delivery, vegan options, wheelchair access, and so on. Customers filtering by their needs see businesses that have set those attributes. The ones that haven't get skipped.
- **NAP consistency** means having the same Name, Address, and Phone across every platform. When Wolt shows one address and Google shows another, Google does not trust the listing. Fixing this is one of the highest-impact local SEO moves.
- **Schema markup (LocalBusiness, Restaurant, FoodEstablishment)** is invisible code on your website that tells Google "this is a restaurant, here are the hours, here is the menu". Google then shows rich results (star rating, hours, menu link) directly in search, which increases clicks.
- **Q&A on Google Business Profile** is a public section where anyone can ask a question. If you don't answer, customers do, often wrongly. Pre-loading owner-marked answers means accurate info reaches every potential customer.
- **Google Business Profile Insights** is Google's free analytics dashboard for your profile. It shows calls, direction requests, photo views, and search queries. We screenshot it before we start and again 4 weeks later, so you have month-on-month proof in numbers.

### A3. Email and WhatsApp Infrastructure

**Price:** 79,966 HUF
**Timeline:** 2 weeks from kickoff

**The problem this solves:**
The audit flagged retention as your single biggest strategic gap. No email list. No WhatsApp broadcast list. No way to bring a Wolt customer back as a higher-margin direct customer. We build the system here. The optional retainer fills it with content every month.

**What we deliver:**

| # | Deliverable |
|---|---|
| 1 | Email provider account set up (MailerLite or Beehiiv free tier). You own the account. |
| 2 | Email capture popup on oliviks.com. First-order incentive of your choice ("Free puff puff with first order" or similar). |
| 3 | Welcome sequence: 3 emails. Email 1: thank you and discount code. Email 2: founders' story. Email 3: invitation to weekly specials. |
| 4 | Weekly specials email template, designed in English and Hungarian, ready to fill in each week. |
| 5 | Order receipt email rebranded. Replaces the default WooCommerce email with a branded version. |
| 6 | GDPR compliance: consent checkbox on capture form, double opt-in flow, unsubscribe link, privacy policy update. |
| 7 | WhatsApp broadcast list set up as a parallel retention channel. Business WhatsApp number, broadcast list created, opt-in flow via website form and QR code at the pickup counter. |
| 8 | WooCommerce integration. Customers automatically added to the email list with double opt-in after their first order. |
| 9 | One-page handover document: how to send a weekly specials email, how to send a WhatsApp broadcast, how to add a subscriber. |

**What these technical bits mean for you:**

- **Welcome sequence** is the set of automated emails that go out the day after someone signs up, then a few days later, then a week later. It turns a one-time visitor into a three-touchpoint relationship without any manual work from you.
- **Double opt-in** means a new subscriber clicks a link to confirm their email before being added to your list. GDPR requires this in Hungary. It also keeps your list clean (no fake addresses) and improves email delivery rates.
- **WooCommerce integration** is the link between your shop and your email list. When someone places an order, they get added to your list automatically (after confirming). Every paying customer becomes a marketing contact for free.
- **WhatsApp broadcast list** is a feature in WhatsApp Business that lets you message many customers at once, with each person receiving it as a private one-to-one message (not a group chat). Open rates on WhatsApp are typically 70 to 90 percent versus 20 to 30 percent on email. For weekly specials, this is the most effective channel.

**What you provide:**
- A business WhatsApp number (or willingness to set one up)
- Privacy policy approval
- One first-subscriber incentive offer you are willing to give

**Not included:**
- Email provider monthly fees if you exceed the free tier (typically 1,000 subscribers). You own the account and the bill.
- Ongoing email writing (covered in the optional retainer)
- WhatsApp Business API integration (the manual broadcast list is sufficient for your current volume)
