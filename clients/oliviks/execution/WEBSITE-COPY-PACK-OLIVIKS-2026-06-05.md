## About Page Draft

### A Nigerian Kitchen in the Heart of Budapest

Oliviks Kitchen was created by Cynthia and Aese, a Nigerian couple who studied for their Masters in Debrecen before building a kitchen in Budapest. They noticed something missing: a place where Nigerians could find the food they grew up with, and where Hungarian and international guests could experience real Nigerian cooking without needing to know the menu already.

Their kitchen started from a simple belief. Food should feel generous. It should carry memory, comfort, and culture. That is why Oliviks serves dishes like jollof rice, egusi soup, suya, fried yam, puff puff, and other Nigerian favorites made fresh for pickup and delivery.

For Nigerian students, workers, and families in Budapest, Oliviks is a taste of home. For first-time guests, it is an easy way to discover one of West Africa's most loved food cultures.

### Our Ingredients

Good Nigerian food depends on bold seasoning, fresh preparation, and careful handling. At Oliviks, we focus on food that is cooked with care, served generously, and explained clearly so every guest knows what they are ordering.

## Product Description Drafts

Use these as first-pass product descriptions. Match exact product names and prices inside WooCommerce before publishing.

### Jollof Rice
A classic West African rice dish cooked in a rich tomato and pepper base with warm spices. It is smoky, savory, and one of the easiest first dishes to try if you are new to Nigerian food.

### Fried Rice
Nigerian-style fried rice with vegetables, seasoning, and a bright, satisfying flavor. A lighter rice option that pairs well with chicken, beef, plantain, or salad.

### Egusi Soup
A thick, rich soup made with ground melon seeds, leafy vegetables, and deeply seasoned meat or fish. Best enjoyed with swallow such as pounded yam or eba.

### Efo Riro
A flavorful Yoruba-style spinach stew cooked with peppers, onions, and traditional seasoning. It is hearty, aromatic, and excellent with rice or swallow.

### Ogbono Soup
A comforting Nigerian soup made from ground ogbono seeds, known for its smooth texture and deep savory flavor. Served with swallow for the full experience.

### Okra Soup
A fresh, comforting soup made with okra, vegetables, and Nigerian spices. It is especially good with eba, pounded yam, or other swallow sides.

### Pounded Yam
A smooth, stretchy Nigerian side served with soups such as egusi, efo riro, ogbono, or okra. It is not eaten alone; it is used to scoop and enjoy the soup.

### Eba
A classic Nigerian swallow made from garri. It has a firm texture and pairs perfectly with rich soups like egusi, ogbono, and okra.

### Amala
A traditional Yoruba swallow with an earthy flavor and soft texture. It is usually served with rich soups and stews.

### Suya Sticks
Nigerian-style grilled meat seasoned with a bold peanut-spice blend. Smoky, spicy, and perfect as a starter or main dish.

### Fried Yam
Golden slices of yam fried until crisp outside and soft inside. A simple, satisfying side that pairs well with pepper sauce, fish, or meat.

### Plantain
Sweet ripe plantain fried until golden and caramelized. It adds a soft, sweet balance to spicy Nigerian dishes.

### Puff Puff
Soft, sweet Nigerian dough balls, fried until golden. A beloved snack or dessert, especially good for first-time guests and families.

### Moi Moi
A steamed bean pudding made from blended beans, peppers, and spices. Soft, savory, protein-rich, and often served as a side with rice dishes.

### Akara
Crispy fried bean cakes made from blended black-eyed peas and spices. A popular Nigerian snack or breakfast item.

### Pepper Soup
A light but powerful Nigerian soup with aromatic spices and heat. Best for guests who enjoy bold, warming flavors.

### Chicken
Tender chicken prepared with Nigerian seasoning and served as a protein add-on or main plate component. Pairs well with jollof rice, fried rice, and plantain.

### Beef
Seasoned beef cooked for rich flavor and served with rice, soups, or sides. A hearty option for guests who want a filling meal.

### Fish
Fish prepared with Nigerian spices and served with rice, yam, plantain, or soup. A strong choice for guests who like bold seasoning and lighter protein.

### Turkey
A rich, satisfying protein option with deep seasoning. Works well with jollof rice, fried rice, or plantain.

### Salad
A fresh side to balance the richness and spice of Nigerian mains. Add it to rice dishes for a lighter plate.

### Extra Stew
A tomato and pepper-based sauce for adding more flavor and heat to your meal. Ideal with rice, yam, plantain, or protein.

### Mixed Plate / Combo
A generous plate combining Nigerian favorites in one order. Best if you want to try several flavors at once.
