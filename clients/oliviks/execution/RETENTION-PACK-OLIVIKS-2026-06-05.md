### Email 2 - Founder Story

Subject:
Why we started Oliviks Kitchen

Preview:
A Nigerian kitchen for Budapest.

Body:
Hi,

Oliviks Kitchen began with Cynthia and Aese, a Nigerian couple who studied for their Masters in Debrecen before building a food business in Budapest. They saw a simple gap: Nigerians were studying, working, and building lives in Hungary, but there were very few places where they could find the food they grew up with.

So Cynthia and Aese built a kitchen around real Nigerian comfort food: jollof rice, egusi soup, suya, fried yam, puff puff, plantain, and more.

For Nigerians, it is a taste of home. For Hungarian and international guests, it is a welcoming first step into Nigerian food.

If you are not sure what to order first, start with jollof rice, plantain, and puff puff. If you want the full traditional experience, try egusi soup with pounded yam.

Warmly,
Oliviks Kitchen
